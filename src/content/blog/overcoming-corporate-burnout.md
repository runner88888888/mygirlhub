If you have ever finished a workday feeling hollow rather than accomplished, you may be experiencing **burnout** — not ordinary fatigue, but a state of emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced efficacy that builds when demands consistently outpace recovery.

## How burnout shows up in high-performing women

Burnout rarely announces itself with a single bad week. More often, it arrives as:

- **Chronic depletion** that sleep does not fix
- **Irritability or numbness** toward work you once cared about
- **Brain fog** during meetings you used to lead with ease
- **Guilt** for resting, as if stillness were a moral failure

Corporate cultures that reward availability over sustainability disproportionately affect women who also carry invisible labour at home. Recognising these patterns is the first act of recovery — not weakness.

## The three pillars of sustainable recovery

### 1. Nervous system safety before optimisation

Before new morning routines or habit stacks, your body needs signals of safety: predictable meals, daylight exposure, and boundaries around after-hours email. Recovery begins when your nervous system believes it is allowed to stand down.

### 2. Boundaries as infrastructure, not personality

Saying "no" once is not a boundary; a boundary is a **repeatable system**. Examples include:

- Auto-responses that set realistic reply windows
- Calendar blocks labelled "non-negotiable recovery"
- Escalation paths that do not default to you for every urgent task

### 3. Micro-restoration throughout the week

Weekend collapse is not recovery. Aim for **daily deposits**: a ten-minute walk between calls, one meal eaten away from your screen, or five minutes of breath-focused pause before transitioning to caregiving roles at home.

## When to seek professional support

If symptoms persist beyond a few weeks, affect sleep or mood significantly, or include hopelessness, please consult a GP or mental health professional. Burnout can overlap with anxiety and depression — support is a strength, not a setback.

## A compassionate reframe

You are not failing at wellness; you may be succeeding at survival in an unsustainable environment. Recovery is not about becoming more resilient to endless pressure — it is about reclaiming a life where your wellbeing is not the last item on the agenda.
